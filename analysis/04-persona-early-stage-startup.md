# Persona 4: Recruiter/Founder at an Early-Stage Startup

## Persona Profile

**Name:** Michał Nowak
**Role:** CTO / Co-founder (does his own hiring)
**Company type:** Early-stage startup (seed to Series A), 5–25 people, based in Poland or with a Polish tech team. Could be a vertical SaaS, marketplace, proptech, edtech, or health-tech venture.
**Business model:** VC-funded or bootstrapped, building an MVP or scaling the first version. Budget is tight, every hire matters enormously.
**What he's hiring for:** Senior Backend Engineer / first senior hire / potential technical co-lead. B2B contract, fully remote or Cracow-based. Needs someone who can build, not just plan.

---

## What Michał Looks For

1. **Versatility** — startup hires can't be single-stack specialists. He needs someone who can do backend, touch frontend, debug infrastructure, and talk to customers when needed
2. **Speed and pragmatism** — "perfect is the enemy of shipped." Can this person deliver working software quickly?
3. **Ownership mindset** — no product manager to write tickets here. The engineer needs to understand the problem and solve it end-to-end
4. **Scaffolding / greenfield experience** — has this person built something from zero before?
5. **Cultural fit** — strong opinions are welcome, toxicity is not. In a team of 5, every personality matters.
6. **Cost-consciousness** — can they build it on a budget? AWS bills matter when you're pre-revenue
7. **Mentoring ability** — if/when they hire juniors, can this person level them up?

## Recommendations for This Persona

### Must-Do

1. **Add a "speed" counterbalance to the quality narrative.** The quality emphasis is strong but one-sided. Add evidence of fast delivery: "Took over after software house failed to deliver and built the product from scratch just in time to meet investor roadmap" — this exists in the Uwazaj.pl section but should be elevated to the executive summary. Something like: "Balances quality with delivery speed — built MVPs from scratch on startup timelines."
2. **Reframe "not a manager" as "technical leadership."** Instead of closing the door on management, say something like: "Focused on technical leadership and hands-on engineering. Can lead a small team technically, though I prefer growing depth over headcount."

### Should-Do

3. **Add explicit AI/LLM project experience** if you have any. If you've integrated OpenAI into a project, built RAG pipelines, or even experimented with LLM-based features — mention it. This is table stakes for many 2025–2026 startups.
4. **Highlight the Uwazaj.pl story more prominently.** "Took over from a failed software house and delivered solo" is one of the strongest startup signals on the CV. Consider making it a key highlight in the executive summary.
5. **Consider adding a "Side Projects" section.** GitHub link is there, but a curated list of 2–3 interesting personal projects would show ongoing curiosity and initiative — things startup founders value highly.

### Nice-to-Have

6. **Keep the personality sections as-is.** For startups, the "Who am I as a person" section is a feature, not a bug. The Warhammer reference, gym habits, and sci-fi collection make you memorable and human. Founders want to work with interesting people.
7. **Add availability / start date.** Startups move fast. "Available to start within 2 weeks" is a competitive advantage over someone in a 3-month notice period.
