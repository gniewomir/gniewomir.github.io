# Persona 2: Recruiter at an International Product Company

## Persona Profile

**Name:** Sarah Chen
**Role:** Senior Technical Recruiter
**Company type:** US or UK-based product company with a Polish engineering office (50–500 engineers), e.g., Hippo Insurance (via SwingDev), Brainly, Allegro-scale companies, or a SaaS scaleup opening a Cracow/Warsaw hub

---

## What Sarah Looks For

1. **Product thinking** — can the engineer understand the "why" behind features, not just the "how"?
2. **Ownership & autonomy** — product companies need engineers who can own a domain, not just execute tickets
3. **System design maturity** — microservices, event-driven architecture, data migrations — signs of scaling experience
4. **Long-term fit** — she's investing in onboarding; she needs 2+ years of tenure ideally
5. **Collaboration skills** — this person will work with product managers, designers, data teams
6. **Tech stack alignment** — important but secondary to thinking quality; they can teach NestJS, they can't teach product sense
7. **Cultural signal** — blog, open source, thoughtful communication style — signals of someone who'll thrive in a product culture

## Recommendations for This Persona

### Must-Do

1. **Quantify impact at every position.** Transform descriptions like "saved a lot of time for agents" into "reduced agent lead-processing time by X%, enabling the team to handle Y% more leads without headcount increase." Even rough estimates are better than nothing. Product companies think in terms of business outcomes.
2. **Reframe the "not a manager" statement.** Instead of defining yourself by what you're not, describe what you are aiming for: "Senior/Staff-track engineer focused on technical leadership, architecture, and domain complexity." This signals growth without management ambitions.

### Should-Do

3. **Expand the Hippo/SwingDev section.** This is your strongest product company signal. Add more detail: what was the scale (how many microservices, team size, data volume)? What architectural decisions did you make and why? What was the underwriting domain redesign's expected impact?
4. **Add a "What I'm Looking For" section.** Product companies want to see alignment. Something like: "Seeking a product-oriented team where I can own a domain, deepen backend architecture, and contribute to technical strategy." This helps Sarah match you to the right team.
5. **Highlight the journalism background more.** This is a genuine differentiator. Product companies increasingly value engineers who can write, communicate clearly, and understand user perspectives. Your journalism experience + blog makes you rare.

### Nice-to-Have

6. **Pin a notable open-source project or blog post** in the GitHub/blog links. Make it easy for Sarah to see your thinking without clicking through.
7. **Add "Scale" indicators** to each role: team size, user count, data volume, request throughput — anything that helps calibrate the complexity you've operated at.
