**Name:** Tomasz Kowalczyk
**Role:** HR Business Partner / IT Recruitment Lead

3. **Enterprise-grade experience** — large-scale systems, security, compliance, audit trails
4. **Process adherence** — Scrum/SAFe experience, documentation culture, JIRA/Confluence
5. **ATS-compatible CV** — clean, keyword-rich, standard format
6. **Seniority consistency** — clear title progression from Junior to Mid to Senior
7. **Team player signals** — less "I" and more "we", signs of working within larger organizational structures
8. **Polish language** — internal communication is almost always in Polish, even if technical docs are in English

### Overall Score: 6.5/10

Tomasz would still **hesitate on this CV** for most enterprise positions. The Technology Summary section is a significant improvement — ATS will now extract skills and years of experience reliably, and the categorization maps well to enterprise job requirement matrices. However, the missing education section remains a hard blocker in many corporate processes, and the ironic subtitle on the Technology Summary would read as dismissive of corporate recruitment norms. The technical substance underneath remains strong enough that with education added and tone adjusted, the candidate would comfortably reach technical interviews.

## Recommendations for This Persona

### Must-Do (If Targeting Enterprise Roles)

1. **Add a formal education section immediately.** University name, field of study, years attended. If you have a degree — list it with honors/GPA if notable. If you attended without completing — list it as "Studies in [field], [University], [years]." If you're self-taught, list notable courses/certifications under "Education & Professional Development." This is non-negotiable for enterprise applications.
2. ~~**Improve ATS keyword density in the existing PDF.**~~ **PARTIALLY DONE.** The new "Technology Summary" section dramatically improves ATS keyword extraction — skills are now categorized, named explicitly, and include years of experience. However, an "Education" section is still missing, which remains the primary blocker for enterprise roles. Additionally, consider removing or neutralizing the subtitle "For the convenience of corporate recruiters & automated CV scanning tools" — in a corporate context it reads as sarcastic toward the process, which won't land well with HR gatekeepers.
3. **Get at least one certification.** AWS Cloud Practitioner or AWS Solutions Architect Associate would take 2–4 weeks of study and dramatically improve enterprise CV scoring. Azure Fundamentals (AZ-900) is even faster.

### Should-Do

4. **Rewrite the tone for corporate audiences.** Replace opinionated statements with neutral professional language:
   - "Drug-resistant microservices" -> "Architecturally appropriate solutions — avoiding premature complexity"
   - "Cargo cults" -> "Evidence-based technical decisions"
   - "I tend to overexplain" -> "Thorough documentation and communication of technical decisions"
5. **Consolidate early career.** Instead of listing freelance contracts, Idium, and Uwazaj.pl separately, consider grouping 2011–2015 as "Early Career: Web Development (Freelance & Agency)" with a brief summary. This reduces the "job-hopper" impression.
6. **Add enterprise methodology keywords.** Even if your Agile experience wasn't "SAFe by the book," mention formal methodology awareness: "Agile (Scrum, Kanban), familiar with SAFe and ITIL principles."
7. **Soften the B2B preference.** Instead of the tax explanation, simply say "B2B or employment" — negotiate the details later.

### Nice-to-Have

8. **Add a "Key Achievements" section** at the top with 3–4 quantified bullets from your best roles (Gremi Media metrics are perfect for this).
9. **Include a professional photo** — still common in Polish enterprise recruitment.
10. **Mention security clearance willingness** if applicable — banks and defense contractors often require this.

### Honest Assessment

Enterprise/corporate roles are likely the **weakest fit** for this candidate's profile and presentation style. The technical substance is there, but the cultural mismatch between the opinionated, independent contractor persona and the consensus-driven, process-heavy enterprise environment is significant. If enterprise roles are a target, substantial CV and positioning rework is needed. If they're not a priority — that's a perfectly reasonable strategic choice.
