# Persona 1: Recruiter at a Polish Software House

## Persona Profile

**Name:** Karolina Mazur
**Role:** IT Recruiter / Talent Acquisition Specialist
**Company type:** Mid-size Polish software house (80–200 people), e.g., Pragmatic Coders, STX Next, Netguru, Brainhub
**Business model:** Body-leasing / team augmentation / project-based outsourcing for foreign clients (mainly DACH, Nordics, UK, US)
**What she's hiring for:** Senior Backend Developer (TypeScript/Node) for a client project, B2B contract, hybrid or remote

---

## What Karolina Looks For

1. **Exact tech stack match** — client said "NestJS + PostgreSQL + TypeScript" so she's filtering for exactly that
2. **Fast onboarding** — the client is paying from day one, so the developer needs to be productive within 1–2 weeks
3. **Client-facing communication skills** — developer will interact directly with the foreign client
4. **English proficiency** — C1 minimum, ideally proven in a work context
5. **B2B availability** — the norm for software houses
6. **References** — ideally from previous similar engagements
7. **Stability** — client projects often run 6–18 months; frequent short stints are a yellow flag

### Overall Score: 8.5/10

Karolina would **confidently shortlist this CV** for backend/Node positions. The tech stack match, international experience, and now the structured Technology Summary make her job easy — she can quickly verify stack alignment and either forward the PDF or extract data into her house template. The remaining half-point deduction is for the opinionated sections that she'd want to discuss before presenting to conservative clients.

## Recommendations for This Persona

### Must-Do

1. ~~**Consider adding a structured skills matrix to the PDF.**~~ **DONE.** The new "Technology Summary" section covers this comprehensively — categorized by domain (Concepts, Languages, Frameworks, DevOps, Databases, Frontend) with years of experience. This is exactly what Karolina needs for quick matching and client-template extraction.
2. **Add an education section.** Even if it's just university name + field + years. DACH clients in particular expect this.
3. **Standardize job titles.** If you were functioning as a senior, say "Senior" consistently. The inconsistency between "Software Engineer", "Backend Developer", "Software Consultant", and "Full Stack Developer" makes it hard to position you.

### Should-Do

4. ~~**Add a skills matrix / technology table.**~~ **DONE.** The Technology Summary section accomplishes this.
5. **Tone down or relocate the philosophy sections.** The content is genuinely thoughtful, but move it to your blog or LinkedIn. For the CV, distill it to 2–3 bullet points under "Approach" that are more neutral: "Quality-focused development with emphasis on testing and maintainability."
6. **Add a brief education/certification section.** Even self-study, completed courses, or AWS/Azure certifications would help.

### Nice-to-Have

7. **Remove the Warhammer reference** ("Archeotech") — it's fun for engineers but confusing for non-technical recruiters and client stakeholders reviewing the CV.
8. **Add quantifiable achievements** to more positions. The Gremi Media section does this well (97.6% to 99.4% uptime, traffic +33%). Other positions would benefit from similar metrics.
