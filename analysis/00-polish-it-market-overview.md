# Polish IT Job Market Overview (2025–2026)

## Context

This document summarizes the current state of the Polish software developer job market, providing context for the persona-based CV analysis that follows.

---

## Market State

The Polish IT market has transitioned from the hiring frenzy of 2021–2022 into a **mature, stable phase**. After a contraction in 2023, job postings grew 8.4% year-over-year in 2025 (based on ~111,000 analyzed offers). However, actual hires grew only 8% despite a 21% increase in recruitment processes — employers are significantly more selective.

- **38% of Polish IT firms** plan to hire in the coming period
- **27% are considering layoffs**
- **32% expect no staffing changes**
- Average job posting attracts **52–57 applications**

## Demand Landscape

| Area                   | Trend     | Notes                                                              |
|------------------------|-----------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Data / Analytics       | **Hot**   | 10.8% of all offers — overtook JavaScript as #1 category           |
| AI / Machine Learning  | **Hot**   | +15% salary increase YoY, record growth                            |
| DevOps / Cloud         | **Strong**| Consistently sought, especially AWS, Azure, K8s                    |
| Backend (Node/TS)      | **Stable**| Solid demand, especially at mid-to-senior level                    |
| Frontend (React, etc.) | **Cooling**| Still present but fewer dedicated roles                            |
| Junior positions       | **Weak**  | Less than 5% of all offers — companies want stack-ready hires      |

## Salary Benchmarks (2025, monthly)

### By Seniority (median)

| Level   | B2B (net/month) | Employment (gross/month) |
|---------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Junior  | ~€2,100 / ~9,000 PLN  | ~€1,860 / ~8,000 PLN  |
| Mid     | ~€4,395 / ~19,000 PLN | ~€3,490 / ~15,000 PLN |
| Senior  | ~€5,760 / ~25,000 PLN | ~€5,015 / ~21,500 PLN |

### Key Observations

- **B2B contracts** offer 13–26% higher net income than employment contracts
- **Senior developers** are the only group whose real earnings slightly exceed advertised rates (+1%)
- **Mid-level** developers face the largest gap — earning 19–21% below advertised upper ranges
- TypeScript/Node/NestJS senior B2B rates typically land in the **22,000–30,000 PLN/month** range

## Work Model Trends

- **Hybrid** work offers grew **29%** — becoming the dominant model
- **Fully remote** is still available but increasingly reserved for senior roles or international contracts
- **On-site** continues to shrink

## Recruitment Process Evolution

1. **ATS filtering** is standard — keywords, experience levels, certifications matter
2. **Asynchronous video interviews** are growing (AI-assessed in some cases)
3. **Multi-stage selection** is the norm — technical tasks, system design, cultural fit
4. **Identity fraud / fake CVs** is a rising concern, leading to stricter vetting
5. **Overemployment** (B2B contractors working multiple clients) is a known risk employers try to mitigate

## Poland's Position Internationally

- **650,000+ IT professionals**, Central Europe's largest tech ecosystem
- Senior developers earn ~30% less than Western Europe, ~66% less than US equivalents
- **Strong English proficiency** (ranked 15th globally)
- **CET/CEST timezone** — 4–6 hours overlap with US East Coast
- **Low attrition** — European tech talent averages 12% turnover, among the lowest globally
- **B2B contracting culture** is well-established and legally straightforward
